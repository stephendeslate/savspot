# Blocked Dates

Blocked dates let you close your calendar for specific days when you are unavailable. Use them for holidays, vacations, team events, or any other time you do not want to accept bookings.

## How Blocked Dates Work

When a date is blocked, all time slots for that day are removed from your booking page. Clients cannot book appointments on blocked dates regardless of your regular availability rules. Existing bookings on a blocked date are not automatically cancelled -- you should reschedule or cancel them manually before blocking the date.

## Blocking a Date

1. Navigate to **Settings** > **Availability**, or go directly to `/settings/availability`.
2. Open the **Blocked Dates** section.
3. Click **Add Blocked Date**.
4. Select the date or date range you want to block.
5. Optionally add a reason (e.g., "Thanksgiving" or "Team Retreat"). The reason is for internal reference only and is not shown to clients.
6. Click **Save**.

## Blocking a Date Range

To block multiple consecutive days (such as a vacation), select both a start date and end date. All days within the range are blocked in a single entry.

| Scenario | How to Block |
|----------|-------------|
| Single holiday | Select one date |
| Week-long vacation | Select start and end date |
| Recurring annual holiday | Create a recurring blocked date |

## Recurring Blocked Dates

For holidays or closures that happen every year, you can mark a blocked date as recurring. Recurring blocked dates automatically apply to the same date each year without manual re-entry.

> **Tip:** Set up your recurring blocked dates at the beginning of the year. This prevents clients from booking on holidays months in advance.

## Blocked Dates and Team Members

Blocked dates can apply to the entire business or to individual staff members. A business-level block closes all bookings for the day. A staff-level block only removes that team member's availability, while other staff remain bookable.

> **Tip:** Check your calendar for existing bookings before blocking a date. Reach out to affected clients to reschedule before the block takes effect.
